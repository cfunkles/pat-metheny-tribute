Pat Metheny

Jazztimes: Best Guitarist of 2016

Winner of 20 Grammy Awards

Here is a brief timeline showing how Pat Metheny became one of the greatest musicians

  • 1954: Born in Kansas City, Missouri on August 12, 1954
  • 1962: Learned to play trumpet
  • 1966: Switched to guitar playing
  • 1969: At age of 15 won a scholoarship for a jazz where he met Attila Zoller, who soon after introduced him to Jim Hall and Ron Carter. A big deal in effecting Metheny's career path.
  • 1972: At age of 18, offered a college teaching position at University of Miami in Coral Gabels FL. Shortly afterwards he moved on to teach as an assistant at the Berklee College of Music learning from the great Gary Burton jazz vibraphonist.
  • 1974: At approximatly age 21 year debuted first recorded album
  • 1975: Entered the professional jazz performance scene playing in Gary Burton's band
    • 1976: Realeased his first album titled Bright Size Life.
  • 1977: At age 23 started the Pat Metheny Group that goes on to recieve 10 Grammys and international recognition
